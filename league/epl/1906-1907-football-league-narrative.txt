The 1906-1907 season was the 19th season of the Football League First Division.

It unfolded as a long, shifting campaign in which every Saturday and every midweek interruption altered the balance of the championship race, the European-style notion of goal difference not yet in place and the older ratio method giving extra weight to the exact shape of scorelines. What mattered week by week was not only who won, but how emphatically they won, who stumbled, and which clubs managed to keep their place near the top as the fixtures accumulated.

What follows is a chronological account of the season as it developed from date to date, with each batch of matches treated as its own chapter in the story and the state of the table made explicit after every set of results.
