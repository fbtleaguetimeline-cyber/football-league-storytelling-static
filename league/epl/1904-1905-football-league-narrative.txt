The 1904-1905 season was the 17th season of the Football League First Division. Played under the old two-points-for-a-win system, it unfolded as a campaign in which the table had to be read almost day by day. From the first scattered fixtures in early September to the closing reckonings of late April, every cluster of results rebalanced the championship argument. What follows is a chronological account of that season, told through the matches themselves and through the standings after every date.

The next date would decide whether that arrangement represented genuine control or merely another temporary turn in a highly changeable season.
